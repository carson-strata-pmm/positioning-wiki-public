# Category Positioning

## Definition
Category positioning is how a company defines its competitive context: what category it belongs to, whether it's creating a new one, and how it differentiates from the alternatives buyers already know. Strata's approach emphasizes owning the frame, not just the features.

## How It Works
Strata identifies one of three positioning stances for each client:

1. **Category creation** -- define a new category and become its first leader.
2. **Category redefinition** -- take an existing category and reframe what it means.
3. **Head-to-head competition** -- compete directly but with a clear differentiator.

The positioning stance drives everything: the hero problem framing, the POV opening, the competitive differentiation section, and even the product naming.

## Examples from Client Work

### Category Creators
| Client | New Category | How Defined |
|--------|-------------|-------------|
| Eudia | Augmented Intelligence Platform (AIP) | Defined "augmented intelligence" as distinct from "artificial intelligence." The platform augments human legal teams with bespoke AI, not generic automation. Also created the "Augmented Law Firm" (ALF) sub-category for outside counsel. |
| TeamOhana | Headcount Management Platform | Carved out a category between FP&A tools (not built for recruiting) and HRIS platforms (not built for finance). Positioned as the only purpose-built solution for the gap. |
| Agency | Superintelligent Customer AI Agent | Positioned Kai as the first "autonomous co-worker" for customer experience, not a copilot or bolt-on AI tool. Drew a line between "bolt-on AI" and "AI-native." |

### Category Redefininers
| Client | Existing Category | Redefinition |
|--------|------------------|--------------|
| BrightEdge | SEO Platform | Proposed redefinition as "Discovery Optimization Platform" or "Search Experience Optimization" to expand beyond traditional SEO into AI-driven search. |
| General Legal | Law Firm | Redefined outside counsel as an "AI-Native Law Firm" that scales elite legal judgment, contrasting with traditional hourly-billing firms. |
| Unity Industry | Real-time 3D / CAD Tools | Repositioned from "game engine for industry" to "the platform where every team can create and collaborate in 3D." Broadened from developers to all stakeholders. |

### Head-to-Head Competitors
| Client | Competitive Frame | Differentiator |
|--------|-------------------|----------------|
| Avoca | vs. point solutions (Broccoli, Netic) and vs. human teams/call centers | End-to-end lifecycle coverage, not just voice AI. Human-in-the-loop. Enterprise-ready. |
| Unity Games | vs. Unreal (3D editors), vs. AppLovin (ad networks) | Full-stack ecosystem (create + deploy + grow), lower barrier to entry, largest dev community. |
| Snappy | vs. transactional gift cards, vs. other platforms (Goody) | Curated global marketplace, customizable campaigns, no sunk cost (pay on redemption). |
| Jane | vs. non-consumption (Gmail, Zoom, Calendly patchwork), vs. purpose-built PMS/EMR (SimplePractice, WebPT) | Flexibility, human support, multi-disciplinary, values-led brand. |

## Patterns & Lessons
- **Category creation requires a named enemy.** Eudia's "augmented intelligence" only works because "artificial intelligence" (off-the-shelf AI) is positioned as the inferior alternative. Agency's "AI-native" only works against "bolt-on AI."
- **The differentiation table is a core deliverable.** Nearly every engagement includes a structured "Old Way vs. New Way" or "Us vs. Them" comparison table. These tables do heavy lifting in sales enablement.
- **Category redefinition is often the safest play.** BrightEdge, General Legal, and Unity Industry all redefine rather than create. This avoids the education burden of a new category while still shifting the buyer's frame.
- **Avoid the category trap.** Several clients (BrightEdge, Unity Industry) needed to avoid being pigeonholed by an outdated category label ("SEO tool," "game engine"). The hero problem framing helps escape the trap by reframing the conversation around outcomes.
- **Two-sided marketplaces need dual positioning.** Eudia's Augmented Law Firm positioning addresses both CLOs (demand side) and law firm partners (supply side) with separate narratives.
